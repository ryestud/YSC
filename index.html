Our Mission

Open the minds and hearts of K through 12 disenfranchised children, young girls, and their families to the world of STEM.

Using a unique Mastery learning program integrating math, chess, coding, financial support and mentoring to help prepare/instill the qualities that will lead to success in life.

Y STEM and Chess Inc. is a means of empowering the most underserved while providing inclusivity lowering the job shortage and reducing poverty.

Our Story

Our current education system was created to satisfy the needs of an Industrial Age not those of the 21st Century. Our society from the latter part of the 1800s to mid-1960s focused on educating everyone to meet the literacy requirements in reading, writing, and math. The problem we face as a society is that our legacy education systems no longer fit the individual learning needs and haven’t since the 1960s that don’t include computers.

Y STEM and Chess Inc. is a 501C3 organization formed to provide a solution to this problem with a focus on those disenfranchised children in our society that have been written off because of social and economic factors. Y STEM and Chess Inc was started by someone who was classified as not “educatable” by the system, but thanks to the determination of he and his family, achieved academic success with a BA in Philosophy and MS degree in Cybersecurity. He was not considered “educatable” above a 3rd or 4th-grade level by the school system. When forming the organization, the founder also recruited people with experience to solve the problem to help develop the YSC learning program. The first is a public school math teacher with over 30 years of experience in low income areas.

What is Mastery


“Mastery” has to do with content in the form of clearly defined (quantified and qualified) Learning Objectives comprised of Tasks, Skills, and Knowledge that must be mastered (100%) before moving on to the next Learning Objective. Anything less than 100% is not Mastery. The Mastery process of learning is individually focused and personally relevant to that student. Testing is primarily a tool to determine if the Learning Objective has been Mastered, and if not what additional support the individual needs to achieve it. Put simply, it is a go/no go testing threshold. The objective is to ensure there are no holes in each student’s learning foundation.
